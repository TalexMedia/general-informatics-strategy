02 The Five Dimensions

How General Informatics Reads On Our Audit Lens

The same five dimensions we walk through in-person, applied to your public presence from the outside.

01
Audience Clarity Partial
Your site speaks clearly to procurement, service bundles, compliance, office locations. What's less clear is which human inside the buying org is being spoken to. A CISO at a regional bank, a superintendent at a school district, and a harbormaster at a port all need different things. The positioning does not yet distinguish.
02
Authenticity & Voice Critical
Your voice is one of the strongest assets GI has. Three books, decades on panels, published opinions. And it is completely absent from the company's content. geninf.com has the voice of a vendor. Your LinkedIn has the voice of an author. The space between those two is where the opportunity is.
03
Storytelling Power Partial
Baton Rouge Chamber, ThreatWatch, Hissho Sushi, these are real, named stories with real transformations. But they are delivered as case-study text: setup, approach, result, in paragraph form. The human drama is implied, not shown. Debbie's quote hints at what the film version could be.
04
Generosity & Value Strong
The three books, the itstrategy.tech blog, and the service depth on geninf.com all signal a brand willing to teach. This is your strongest dimension. The gift here is the video form of what you are already publishing in print.
05
Consistency & Systems Partial
Blog cadence on itstrategy.tech is solid. Book cadence is impressive (one every two to three years). But the flagship brand and its social channels are inconsistent, no visible weekly heartbeat, no scheduled video, no recurring franchise. A trust brand needs a drumbeat.
03 The Pattern

Two Voices That Don't Meet Yet

The thread that connects everything above.

General Informatics has two things most MSPs never get: a CEO with a real personal brand, and a company with real customer stories. Three published books. Board appointments. A distinguished alumni award. Case studies with named customers and specific outcomes.

But they live on separate shelves. The company's content is written for procurement. Your authority is written for readers and audiences. The space in between, where a buyer in a regulated vertical watches a short video and decides "these are the people I trust with my infrastructure," is empty.

This is the opportunity. Not more content. Video that connects the two voices into one.

04 The Gaps

Three Opportunities, In Priority Order

Each of these is a gap today and a compounding advantage once closed.

Critical
No Founder Voice On Camera
You are the most valuable authority asset General Informatics has, and you are not on video anywhere. For a 3x author, board-appointed, executive-accomplished CEO, this is the single largest credibility gap we found. Security and IT buyers do not buy bundles. They buy the people behind them.
Critical
Customer Stories Trapped In Text
Debbie Hightower's quote would be a 60-second film with an order-of-magnitude higher emotional payload. Baton Rouge Chamber, ThreatWatch, and Hissho Sushi are real transformations sitting in paragraph form. Each one is a case-study film waiting to be shot.
Moderate
Authority Platforms Disconnected
Your books, LinkedIn, itstrategy.tech, and geninf.com are all building authority, but each for itself. A single video series, anchored on you, becomes the hub all four platforms can point to.

07 For You

The Full Build For General Informatics

The video above is one episode. Here's the rest of what we'd make alongside it, tied back to the gaps in section 04.

1
The CEO Series · 12 Episodes a Year

The monthly franchise that "Why Trust Isn't a Feature" pilots. Don in studio, one topic per episode, translating a perspective from the Enhanced trilogy for an MSP or IT audience.

  • 12 episodes per year, ~10 minutes each, batched (4 episodes per studio day, 3 shoot days per year)
  • Topics sourced from your books, LinkedIn activity, and conversations with your sales team
  • Distribution: geninf.com hero, new YouTube channel, Don's LinkedIn + GI page, 3-4 clipdowns per episode for IG and X
  • Closes gap 01 (founder voice) and gap 03 (authority platforms disconnected)
2
Customer Story Films · 3 to 4 Per Year

Customer on camera, telling their before-and-after as a narrative arc. Not testimonial-as-quote.

  • Start with Debbie at LeBlanc Pediatrics, the homepage testimonial already becomes the hook
  • Next: the Baton Rouge Chamber "Smart Office" story as a visual transformation piece
  • ~2 minutes each, full production, filmed on-location with the customer
  • Distribution: service landing pages, sent by sales pre-call, emailed post-demo
  • Closes gap 02 (customer stories trapped in text)
3
Vertical Explainers · 60 to 90 Seconds, One Per Vertical

Not sales spots. Short "here is how we think about risk in your world" pieces, one per vertical.

  • Six total: banking, government, healthcare, manufacturing, transportation, education/maritime
  • Filmed in one production block, then used across outbound, vertical landing pages, and sales follow-up
  • Each one opens with a vertical-specific truth, not a product feature
4
Thought Leadership Integration

Pull the authority you're building on itstrategy.tech back into the flagship brand.

  • Monthly essay paired with a 5-minute video version, both published on geninf.com
  • Cross-link both directions so itstrategy.tech readers find the video and geninf.com visitors find the depth
  • Closes gap 03 (authority platforms disconnected)
